Completing the Square to Find the Turning Point

Exam Question

Consider the expression $-5 - 6x - x^2$.

(a) Write the expression in the form $q-(x+p)^2$.

(b) Hence write down the coordinates of the turning point of $y=-5-6x-x^2$.

Solution

First write the quadratic in standard form.

$$y = -x^2 - 6x - 5$$

Factor out $-1$ from the quadratic terms.

$$y = -(x^2 + 6x) - 5$$

Complete the square.

$$x^2 + 6x = (x+3)^2 - 9$$

Substitute this into the expression.

$$y = -[(x+3)^2 - 9] - 5$$

Simplify.

$$y = 4 - (x+3)^2$$

The turning point is therefore $(-3,4)$.
