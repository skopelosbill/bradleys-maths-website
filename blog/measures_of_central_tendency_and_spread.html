Measures of Central Tendency and Spread

GCSE all examination boards and IGCSE Cambridge (0580)

Exam Question

Annabelle and Ben both enjoy Ten-Pin Bowling.

These are their scores in their last ten games.

Annabelle: $150, 155, 148, 152, 158, 160, 151, 143, 158, 153$

Ben: $120, 180, 141, 192, 119, 163, 127, 165, 191, 130$

a) Calculate the mean of each set of scores.

b) Calculate the median of each set of scores.

c) Calculate the range of each set of scores.

d)Calculate the inter-quartile of each set of scores.

e) Use your answers to compare and contrast the two bowlers.

Before you move on to the solution or watch the video, why don't you have a go at this one yourself?

Solution

a) The mean is the sum of each set of scores divided by the number of scores.

Annabelle:

$$\barx_A=\dfrac{150 + 155 + 148 + 152 + 158 + 160 + 151 + 143 + 158 + 153}{10}=\dfrac{1528}{10}=152.8$$

Ben:

$$\barx_B=\dfrac{120 + 180 + 141 + 192 + 119 + 163 + 127 + 165 + 191 + 130}{10}=\dfrac{1528}{10}=152.8$$

b) To find the median you should always put the data in numerical order. With just 10 data items this is easy to do, but with a large number of data items it is best to use a stem and leaf diagram to order them. With an even number of data points, the median is the mean of the middle two

Annabelle:

$$143, 148, 150, 151, 152, 153, 155, 158, 158, 160. M_A=\dfrac{152+153}{2}=152.5$$

Ben:

$$119, 120, 127, 130, 141, 163, 165, 180, 191, 192. M_B=\dfrac{141+163}{2}=152$$

c) The range is the largest data item - the smallest data item in each case

Annabelle:

$$R_A=160-143=17$$

Ben:

$$R_B=192-119=73$$

d) The inter-quartile range (IQR) is $Q_3-Q_1$. $Q_3 \text {is the data item at }\dfrac{3}{4}\text{ of the way through the data and }Q_1\text{is }\dfrac{1}{4}\text{ of the way through the data. These are the mean of the 7th and 8th and the mean of the 2nd and 3rd data points.

Annabelle:

$$IQR_A=\dfrac{155+158}{2}-\dfrac{148+150}{2}=156.5-149=7.5$$

Ben:

$$IQR_B=\dfrac{165+180}{2}-\dfrac{120+127}{2}=172.5-125=47.5$$

e) The mean of each bowler is identical and the medians are also very close. However the range of the data is much more spread out for Ben and this would seem to indicate a lack of consistency in his bowling. When we are comparing the IQR, we are only looking at the middle $50%$ of the data, that is the data clustered around the median. Annabelle's IQR is low at just 7.5 and this indicates a reasonable level of consistency in her score in this middle $50%$. With his IQR of 47.5, indicating a spread of more than six times that of Annabelle it is clear that even in this reduced data set Ben is a very inconsistent bowler in comparison to Annabelle.

Topics covered in this question

  • Mean of ungrouped data
  • Median of ungrouped data
  • Finding the quartiles
  • Finding the range
  • Finding the interquartile range
  • Using the mean, median, range and IQR to compare and contrast two data sets
